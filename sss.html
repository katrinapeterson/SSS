Students Saving Salmon!

We are a team of Edmonds-Woodway High School students dedicated to protecting our local salmon population that pass through the streams in the Edmonds area. We conduct monthly monitoring of the water quality and provide community outreach to inform residents how to best protect the streams.

News:

Release of Juvenile Coho Salmon into Shell Creek:

In late May, students released juvenile coho that they helped
Sound Salmon Solutions raise at the Willow Creek Salmon Hatchery.
They were netted out of the hatchery pond, placed in ice chests,
and transported to Shell Creek. The Coho that survive 2 years
in the ocean will come back to Shell Creek as adults and spawn.

My Edmonds News

Habitat Restoration Project:

By planting native plants around the streams, SSS members hope to
stabilize the streambanks. This will help to prevent erosion, provide
shade to keep the stream cool, and provide a habitat for the insects that
salmon feed on.
